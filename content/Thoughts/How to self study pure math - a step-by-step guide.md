I found this video particularly interesting and useful to me even though I am not a "pure math" person in the slightest. I am peering into a community for which I just barely qualify as educated in, but my enjoyment of the subject matter remains the same.

More importantly, I found some resources from this video to be quite useful for my field of study. I will not be using all the mentioned fields in the video, but **Linear Algebra** is one of the most vital concepts for machine learning and neuroscience alike. Practically everything seems to be represented by vectors, and this knowledge becomes all the more useful when dabbling in **dynamical systems theory**(very important in [[Computational Neuroscience]], although more differential equations than linear algebra).

Beyond this, I am also someone who takes self-study to the extreme. For whatever reasons, I am rather poor in my ability to retain information when it is forced upon me through a class. I find that I excel in these same fields that I have classes for, but only when I seek that knowledge out on my own time. This will become more useful in graduate school, but has hindered my performance in many ways for my undergrad.

## Resources from Video Description

**REAL ANALYSIS**  

Book: “Understanding Analysis” by Stephen Abbott.  
Videos: Lectures by Francis Su (https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL0E754696F72137EC)  

**LINEAR ALGEBRA**  

Book: “Linear Algebra Done Right” by Sheldon Axler  
Videos: Sheldon Axler’s Playlist  
(https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLGAnmvB9m7zOBVCZBUUmSinFV0wEir2Vw)  

**POINT SET TOPOLOGY**  

Online Notes with Problems: MAT327 Course Notes (http://www.math.toronto.edu/ivan/mat327/?resources)  

**COMPLEX ANALYSIS**  

Intro Book: “Visual Complex Functions: an Introduction with Phase Portraits” by Elias Wegert  
More Technical Book: “Complex Analysis” by Serge Lang  
Videos: Wesleyan University Playlist (https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL\_onPhFCkVQjdQTbG0eQk42eH0RaBoYJf)  

**GROUP THEORY**  

Book: “Topics in Algebra” by Herstein (Chapter 2)  
Videos: Lectures by Benedict Gross (https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLelIK3uylPMGzHBuR3hLMHrYfMqWWsmx5)  

**GALOIS THEORY**  

Notes by Tom Leinster: https://www.maths.ed.ac.uk/~tl/gt/gt.pdf  

**DIFFERENTIAL GEOMETRY**  

Book: Introduction to Differentiable Manifolds and Riemannian Geometry by Boothby  

**ALGEBRAIC TOPOLOGY**  

Book: Algebraic Topology by Allen Hatcher (available for free on his website: https://pi.math.cornell.edu/~hatcher/AT/ATpage.html)  
Videos: Lectures by Pierre Albin (https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL41FDABC6AA085E78)
