Transparency is essential for real sustainability, and while China's shift toward a non-fossil fuel dependent future is promising, it is just as important for citizens to be informated and participate in the environmental initiatizes. Another important thing to consider regarding China's censorship is how it limits the ability for outside actors to assess the state of the environment in China. The lack of transparency makes it difficult to hold the government accountable for its environmental policies and practices. This is a significant barrier to achieving real sustainability, as it prevents citizens and outside actors from understanding the true state of the environment and the effectiveness of government initiatives. Overall, while China's shift toward a non-fossil fuel dependent future is promising, it is essential for the government to prioritize transparency and public engagement in order to achieve lasting environmental change.
