John Sever, a visionary from the 1800s, was a man afflicted with what is now known as dissociative identity disorder. Fascinated by the distinct consciousnesses within his own mind, he became obsessed with the idea of artificially recreating the experience. His theories, radical for their time, laid the groundwork for what would eventually become the Severance brain implant. Designed to divide a person’s identity between work and personal life, the implant was inspired by Sever’s own fragmented existence, turning his condition into a blueprint for one of the most controversial advancements in neuroscience.

From an excerpt of the *John Sever Archives*

>  In the dim glow of an oil lamp, John Sever hunched over his desk, scribbling feverishly in his worn leather notebook. His theories on identity and consciousness had consumed him, each inked line a step closer to his life’s work. Across from him, his assistant, a skeptical young doctor, peered over the chaotic notes.
> 
> "You’re saying… this device would allow a man to live two lives? To sever himself from his own burdens?"
> 
> Sever looked up, his eyes alight with the fervor of revelation. He tapped his temple. "Not sever… severance."

## Biography

 **John Sever: The Man Behind Severance**  

### Early Life

John Sever was born in 1836 in New Carthage, a small industrial town known for its early experiments in mechanical automation. From a young age, Sever displayed an unusual fascination with the nature of consciousness, often writing of his experiences with "voices" and "selves" that occupied his mind. Modern historians speculate that he suffered from dissociative identity disorder, a condition that would later inspire his life’s work.  

### The Birth of Severance

As Sever grew older, he became obsessed with the idea of splitting consciousness—allowing individuals to exist as separate selves without memory of their other lives. His experiments, considered radical and even dangerous for his time, involved early forms of electrotherapy and crude surgical interventions. His notes, preserved in a hidden archive, detail a prototype device designed to artificially induce identity separation. Though dismissed as madness in his era, his work would later influence the Lumon Corporation’s controversial Severance procedure.  

### Connection to Lumon

It is rumored that Kier Eagan himself, the enigmatic founder of Lumon, stumbled upon Sever’s research decades later. Some believe that Lumon’s modern brain implant is a direct evolution of Sever’s early theories, while conspiracy theorists claim that remnants of Sever’s consciousness persist within the company’s secretive inner workings.  

### Legacy

While John Sever’s name faded into obscurity, his ideas lived on—reshaping the future in ways he could never have imagined. Today, his legacy remains a whispered mystery, a man who sought to split the soul and, in doing so, may have changed the world forever.
