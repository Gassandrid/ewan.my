## Unit Vectors

The first one to know is the unit vector pointing to the right with a length of **1**:

$$
\hat{i}
$$

And the second one is the unit vector pointing straight up, also with a length of 1:

$$
\hat{j}
$$

These are the **unit vectors** for the $x$ and $y$ coordinates. Another way to think of them is as their matrix notation counterparts:

$$
\hat{i} = \begin{bmatrix}
1  \\
0 \\
0
\end{bmatrix}, \quad \hat{j} = \begin{bmatrix}
0 \\
1 \\
0
\end{bmatrix}
$$
