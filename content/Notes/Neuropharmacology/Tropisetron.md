Dual [[5-HT3 receptor|5-HT3]] antagonist and [[α7 nAChR]] partial agonist. Nicotine's addiction and dopaminergic drive come from its [[α4β2 nAChR|α4β2]] bias, so a selective α7 partial agonist sidesteps that entirely. GTS-21 validated the α7 mechanism in healthy men: working memory, episodic memory, and sustained attention all improved [@kitagawaSafetyPharmacokineticsEffects2003; @vanhaarenGTS21MixedNicotinic1999]. Tropisetron replicates and extends these effects in primates and schizophrenic patients [@callahanTropisetronSensitizesA72017; @xiaOnedayTropisetronTreatment2020].

The key mechanistic distinction from GTS-21 is that GTS-21 drives α7 into a desensitized state. Tropisetron behaves more as a positive allosteric modulator, sensitizing the receptor to endogenous [[acetylcholine|ACh]] rather than fully occupying the orthosteric site [@callahanTropisetronSensitizesA72017]. That's why adding cholinergic tone (nicotine, ALCAR) enhances rather than competes with its effects: you're providing more substrate for a receptor that's now better at responding to it.

The 5-HT3 axis is doing real work independently. 5-HT3 antagonism is pro-cognitive across multiple models [@arnstenEffects5HT3Receptor1997; @barnesEffectsOndansetron5HT31990; @sridhar5HT3ReceptorsSelective2002], likely via disinhibition of [[ACh]] release in [[prefrontal cortex|prefrontal]] and [[hippocampus|hippocampal]] circuits. It's also anxiolytic [@olivier5HT3ReceptorAntagonists2000; @lecrubierRandomizedDoubleblindPlacebocontrolled1993], antidepressant [@rajkumarAuspiciousRole5HT32010], and the class as a whole (vortioxetine, ondansetron) shows durable cognitive and affective benefits without tolerance [@fakhfouri5HT3ReceptorAntagonists2019]. There's also a clinical signal for OCD: tropisetron augments fluvoxamine in treatment-refractory patients [@shalbafanFluvoxamineCombinationTherapy2019], which fits mechanistically given 5-HT3's role in compulsive circuitry.

not only that, but tropisetron activates [[SIRT1]] [@mirshafaTropisetronProtectsBrain2020], NAD-dependent deacetylase responsible for the mediation of a lot of the downstream neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory machinery. adds a longevity angle that's independent of the receptor pharmacology. And 5-HT3 blockade in the liver context improves obesity-associated fatty liver disease [@haubSerotoninReceptorType2011], so there's metabolic benefit that's easy to overlook when thinking about this purely as a nootropic. Dopaminergic at doses below 10mg, antidopaminergic above that [@leeTropisetronReviewIts1993].

The constipation is the main limiting factor. 5-HT3 blockade in the gut tanks motility [@leeTropisetronReviewIts1993], and it's dose-dependent and cumulative. Intranasal administration dramatically cuts GI exposure while preserving CNS effects, since you're bypassing first-pass gut exposure entirely. Oral is current; IN is the preferred route when available. Slow-release magnesium malate manages motility better than high-dose choline. Half-life is around 6 hours but functional effects outlast that. Effective range is 5-10mg.

**Stacks**: Nicotine (α7 synergy, extends and enhances [@callahanTropisetronSensitizesA72017]), ALCAR (cholinergic tone, enhances memory effects), caffeine/PDE inhibitors ([[cAMP]] potentiates antidepressant effects), [[Usmarapride]] (5-HT3 antagonist + 5-HT4 agonist pairing for GI management)
