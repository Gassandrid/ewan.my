also known as **SNIC**, is a special case bifurcation, where a [[Stable Manifold|stable]] node and a [[Saddle fixed point]] collide and annihilate on a closed invariant curve in a planar system, creating a limit cycle with an infinite period.

This is the primary mechanism for how class 1 [[Neuron|neurons]] transition from a resting state to a continous periodic spiking pattern in models like [[Morris-Lecar Model]] and [[Hodgkin Huxley Model]].
