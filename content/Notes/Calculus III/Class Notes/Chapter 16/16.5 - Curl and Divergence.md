Two important operations on vector fields are **curl and divergence**. We will define them in terms of the nabla operator $\nabla$:

$$
\nabla = \left\langle  \frac{\partial}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial}{\partial y}, \frac{\partial}{\partial z}  \right\rangle 
$$

**Note the difference between $\nabla$ and $\nabla f$:** $\nabla$ merely contains the operations of differentiation, and we define:

$$
\nabla f = \left\langle \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial z} \right\rangle
$$

### Curl and Divergence Are Defined As:

> [!Warning] Defintion
>
> $$
> Curl: \ \ \ \ curl(\vec{F}) = \nabla \times \vec{F}
> $$
>
> $$
> Divergence: \ \ \ \ div(\vec{F}) = \nabla \cdot \vec{F}
> $$

Curl is a vector quantity, and divergence is a scalar quantity. If we write $\vec{F} = \langle P(x,y,z), Q(x,y,z), R(x,y,z) \rangle$, then the curl written in component form is:

$$
\begin{align*}
curl(\vec{F}) = \nabla \times \vec{F} &= \begin{vmatrix}
i & j & k \\
\frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\
P & Q & R
\end{vmatrix} \\ \\
&= i\left( \frac{\partial}{\partial y}R - \frac{\partial}{\partial z}Q \right)-j\left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x}R - \frac{\partial}{\partial z}P \right) + k\left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x}Q-\frac{\partial}{\partial y}P \right) \\ \\ 
&= \langle R_{y}-Q_{z}, P_{z}-R_{x}, Q_{x}-P_{y} \rangle
\end{align*}
$$

>[!Abstract] Observe
> The z-component of curl is $Q_{x}-P_{y}$, a quantity we have seen several times now.

In component form, the divergence of $\vec{F}$ is:

$$
\begin{align*}
div(\vec{F}) &= \nabla \cdot \vec{F} = \langle \frac{\partial}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial}{\partial y}, \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \rangle \cdot \langle P,Q,R \rangle \\
&= P_{x} + Q_{y} + R_{z}
\end{align*}
$$

We will discuss the interpretation of the curl and the divergence later in the lecture. For now, we will see how to compute them, and investigate some of their properties.

>[!Success] Solution

> $$
> \begin{align*}
> curl \vec{F} &= \nabla \times \vec{F} = \begin{vmatrix}
> i & j & k \\
> \frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\
> x^2y & 2y^3 & 3z

> &= \langle (3z)_{y}-(2y^3z)_{z}, (x^2y)_{z} - (3z)_{x}, (2y^3z)_{x} - (x^2y)_{y} \rangle \\

> \end{align*}
> $$
