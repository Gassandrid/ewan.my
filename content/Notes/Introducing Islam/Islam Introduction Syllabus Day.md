## Word Nerds

The words we use to talk about religion really matter. **Lots of people**(most American adults, statistically) make big mistakes about word that have to do with Islam.

> [!Abstract] Key Idea
> **Religion** is what people **do**

### What is Islam?

Lots of thing, actually.

Mostly: one way roughly 2 billion people express their culture, belief history, ethics , and identities.

A key property of this is that everyone values different things at a different scale. There is a nuance.

>[!Abstract] Main Definitions
>  **Islam**: the *religion*
> **Muslim**: a *person* who follows Islam
> **Allah:** the name for *a* God in Arabic
> **Qur'an:** Islam's holiest text

#### Some Denominations

>[!Abstract] Main Denominations
> **Sunni**: a denomination (or group) of Muslims represents the majority of Muslims $80\%$
> **Shi'i**: a denomination of Muslims, represents the largest minority of Muslims ($18\%$)

#### Arab Vs Arabic

An **Arab** is an ethnicity and a group of people. **Arabic** is a language.

Most Muslims in the world live in Asia, roughly $66\%$.

Most Muslims are **not** Arabs, nor is Arabic their primary language.

### The United States

Muslims speak all kinds of languages, represent many ethnicities, and are ==The most diverse religion in the USA!==.
