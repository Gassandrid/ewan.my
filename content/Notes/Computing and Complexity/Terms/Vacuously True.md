A statement is "vacuously true" **if it resembles a material conditional statement , where the antecedent**. **is known to be false**.

A vacuous truth is a conditional or universal statement that is true because the antecedent cannot be satisfied. It is sometimes said that a statement is vacuously true because it does not really say anything.

*"All cell phones in the room are turned off" is true when there are no cell phones in the room.*
